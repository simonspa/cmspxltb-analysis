 524    ''' 
 525    General Broken Lines Trajectory. 
 526    ================================ 
 527     
 528    For a track with an initial trajectory from a prefit of the 
 529    (2D, 4D or 5D) measurements (internal seed) or an external  
 530    prediction(external seed) the description of multiple scattering 
 531    is added by offsets in a local system. Along the initial 
 532    trajectory points are defined with can describe a measurement 
 533    or a (thin) scatterer or both. The refit provides corrections 
 534    to the local track parameters (in the local system) and the  
 535    corresponding covariance matrix at any of those points. 
 536   
 537    The broken lines trajectory is defined by (2D) offsets at the  
 538    first and last point and all points with a scatterer. The 
 539    prediction for a measurement is obtained by interpolation of 
 540    the enclosing offsets and for triplets of adjacent offsets 
 541    kink angles are determined. This requires for all points the 
 542    jacobians for propagation to the previous and next offset. 
 543   
 544    Additional local or global parameters can be added and the 
 545    trajectories can be written to special binary files for 
 546    calibration and alignment with Millepede-II. 
 547    (V. Blobel, NIM A, 566 (2006), pp. 5-13). 
 548   
 549    The conventions for the coordinate systems follow: 
 550    Derivation of Jacobians for the propagation of covariance 
 551    matrices of track parameters in homogeneous magnetic fields 
 552    A. Strandlie, W. Wittek, NIM A, 566 (2006) 687-698. 
 553     
 554    Calling sequence: 
 555    ================= 
 556      1. Create trajectory:: 
 557              traj = GblTrajectory() 
 558      2. For all points on initial trajectory  
 559          - Create point (supply jacobian from previous point):: 
 560              point = GblPoint(jacobian) 
 561          - Optionally add measurement to point::     
 562              point.addMeasurement(..) 
 563          - Optionally additional local or global parameters for measurement::  
 564              point.addLocals(..) 
 565              point.addGlobals(..) 
 566          - Optionally add scatterer to point::     
 567              point.addScatterer(..) 
 568          - Add point (ordered by arc length) to trajectory, get label of point::   
 569              label = traj.addPoint(point) 
 570      3. Optionally add external seed:: 
 571              traj.addExternalSeed(..) 
 572      4. Fit trajectory, bet Chi2, Ndf (and weight lost by M-estimators):: 
 573              [..] = traj.fit() 
 574      5. For any point on inital trajectory 
 575          - Get corrections and covariance matrix for track parameters:: 
 576              [..] = traj.getResults(label)  
 577      6. Optionally write trajectory to MP binary file:: 
 578              traj.milleOut(..) 
 579               
 580    Alternatively trajectories can by read from MP binary files and fitted.  
 581    As the points on the initial trajectory are not stored in this files results at 
 582    points (corrections, covariance matrix) are not available. 
 583     
 584    References: 
 585    ===========   
 586      - V. Blobel, C. Kleinwort, F. Meier, 
 587        Fast alignment of a complex tracking detector using advanced track models, 
 588        Computer Phys. Communications (2011), doi:10.1016/j.cpc.2011.03.017 
 589      - C. Kleinwort, General Broken Lines as advanced track fitting method,
